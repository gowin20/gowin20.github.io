16 Interlocking Triangles

A wireframe model made using 48 strips of paper. The model is composed of 16 individual triangles with 3 strips each. To assemble this, I had to weave together individual units in a complicated interlocking pattern.


Alternate view of the weaving pattern. The whole model holds itself together because each triangle pushes against the others.

To nerd out a little bit more: The model has octahedral and tetrahedral symmetry. This means that the model can be represented as an octahedron, with two tetrahedra inlaid within it. Each point where several triangles intersect (look at the center of the above image) is one vertex of these shapes. It's a little easier to understand in person, I promise!

Tricolor Icosahedron

Uses 30 strips of paper, folded into simple modules and interwoven. Note how no color touches itself!




Icosahedron Kusudama

Kusudamas are spherical shapes with cool designs on each face. This one is a platonic solid, meaning each face is identical, and every angle is the same as well. Uses 60 sheets of paper.
